PERSONAL AND NON-PERSONAL INFORMATION

Our Privacy Policy identifies how we treat your personal and non-personal information in compliance with industry standards.

WHAT IS NON-PERSONAL INFORMATION AND HOW IS IT COLLECTED AND USED?

Non-personal information is information that cannot identify you. If you visit this website to read information, such as information about our remodeling marketing services, we may collect certain non-personal information about you from your computer's web browser. Because non-personal information cannot identify you or be tied to you in any way, there are no restrictions on the ways that we can use or share non-personal information.

WHAT IS PERSONAL INFORMATION AND HOW IS IT COLLECTED?

Personal information is information that identifies you as an individual, such as your name, business name, mailing address, email address, telephone number, and company information. We may collect personal information from you in a variety of ways:

  • When you send us an application or consultation request form
  • When you schedule a consultation with us or our team
  • When we collect information about you in support of our marketing services
  • In some places on this website you have the opportunity to send us personal information about yourself, to elect to receive particular information about remodeling marketing strategies, to access our resources, or to participate in webinars or educational activities

ARE COOKIES OR OTHER TECHNOLOGIES USED TO COLLECT PERSONAL INFORMATION?

Yes, we may use cookies and related technologies, such as web beacons, to collect information on our website. A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard disk by a web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you.

One of the primary purposes of cookies is to provide a convenience feature to save you time. The purpose of a cookie is to tell the web server that you have returned to a specific page. For example, if you register for our kitchen remodeling marketing resources, a cookie helps Remodel Booked to recall your specific information on subsequent visits. This simplifies the process of accessing your customized marketing materials and business-specific resources.

We may use web beacons to count visitors to our web pages or to monitor how remodeling professionals navigate our website, and we may include web beacons in email messages to track engagement with our educational content.

Third party vendors also may use cookies on our website. For instance, we may contract with third parties who will use cookies on our website to track and analyze anonymous usage and volume statistical information from our visitors and members. Such information is shared externally only on an anonymous, aggregated basis.
